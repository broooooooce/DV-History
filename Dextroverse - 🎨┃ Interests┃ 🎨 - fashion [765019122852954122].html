An Eskintroduction to Fashion Brands/culture: Spring Court - smalltime French fashion house, pretty underrated Filling Pieces - mid-level [financially] brand renown for their unique silhouette designs GREATS - great minimalist brand @ affordable prices in contrast to well renown but expensive minimalist brands such as Common Projects Bapestas [Bape brand] - the iconic Tokyo fashion house and the shoes that first defined Japanese streetwear White Mountaineering - Japanese fashion house that has frequent collabs with Adidas, highly rec APC - minimalist ACW [A Cold Wall] - minimalist Frank and Oak - classic style brand @ affordable prices Gustins - affordable minimalist brand Twins for Peace Wings and Horns - high level minimalist fashion brand Alexander McQueen - one of the greatest American designer brands, quite pricey but unparalleled quality Beckett Simonon - crowdsourced based shoes, extremely pricey quality at affordable prices but you'll wait upwards of 3 months for them to ship [this is due to the fact they only start production once enough orders are placed] Golden Goose - pricey Italian fashion house but some of the best designs I've ever seen (edited)
---------------- Expensive fashion houses that are worth every penny if you can find them on sale; LV, Prada, Christian Louboutin, Jimmy Choo, JW Anderson, YSL/Yves-Saint Laurent, Common Projects, Karl Lagerfeld, Balenciaga, JM Weston [gentlemenly brand for wearing with suits, etc. If you can find a used pair, highly recommended.] (avoid Gucci, the quality is shit), (avoid Philip Plein - universally considered one of the worst designers of all time and an embarrassment to the German people just a few pegs below Adolf Hitler), Roberto Cavalli [struggling in business so can prolly find some steals], Dior [esp. the Dior x Jordan collab], Givenchy, Maison Margiela [renown for redesigning the shoe of the German army in the 1970's] ---------------- Y3 - Pricey Japanese fashion house by one of the greatest Japanese streetwear designers, Yohji Yamamoto Axel Arigato - minimalist brand, can be pricey but is worth if you can find on sale Red Wings - the best quality boots you can buy aside from YSL Wyatts, but quite pricey Flower Mountain - Bulky, floral-esque shoes in a wide array of colorways Gola - Affordable, decently styled trainers Jak - Mid-level, minimalist brand Oliver Cabell - gr8 mid-level minimalist brand (edited)
Eye-Found - Decent style, affordable Rombaut - Decently expensive Parisian fashion brand that specializes in bulky shoes They New York - Great smalltime NY fashion brand specializing in two-tone minimalist sneakers Shoes to avoid as they are notorious for being "memey" - Fila Disruptor 2 [the choice for every breathing female in Europe], Adidas Stan Smiths, Lacoste [embarrassing], AF1's [yes, they are a meme], Cole Haan [shit quality], Emporio Armani [I’m not gonna even comment on this joke of a fashion house that is the “go-to” brand for smelly Middle Easterners and Europeans who spray cologne on themselves 13 times, and Indians], Onitsuka Tigers [some fashion enthusiasts love these, I think they have some of the ugliest designs in history], Giuseppe Zanotti [good god, these are horrid], Aldo [no, these aren’t even that bad, but they’re so shit quality, you might as well shop at Zara], Rick Owens [this brand is BELOVEDDDDD by fashion enthusiasts - I think it's fucking ugly as dirt] Best sites for browsing and finding gr8 deals; Lyst.com Farfetch.com Endclothing.com Grailed.com Stockx.com Ssense.com (edited)
ABOVE ALL, remember - fashion is PERSONAL! Curate YOUR style! Buy what YOU like and can afford! Don't feel need to conform or care for brand names if you don't! Fashion is what you make it!
