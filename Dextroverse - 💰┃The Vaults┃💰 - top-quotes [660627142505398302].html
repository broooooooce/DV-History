Few things here and there in the morgue squealed at times and came undone, the floor one of them since their makeshift shelter had prolonged vacancy— and before A-Qing and Xiao XingChen moved in with a wounded, unfamiliar man in tote. Momentarily, she checked over her shoulder for the man. He possessed the nimbleness and cunning of a black cat. The intruder often watched A-Qing, and A-Qing performed radiantly with her bamboo cane and the silliness of a reckless, blind young girl. A-Qing felt her heart pound, seeing hide nor tail of the intrusive stranger. She propped her bamboo pole to the rickety oak table and smoothed her palms down the front of her skirt, now dirtied with grime. Daozhang mentioned earlier something about returning a basketful of germinated potatoes to a vendor who misled him once more. The stranger accompanied him, apparently -- and as much as A-Qing distrusted him, and as malicious and offensive as she found him, the stranger owned a valuable quality in haggling. He even received supplies for free with a persuading wave of a blade and a few sultry threats. (edited)
Still, with a grin, she sank to her knees and drove the floorboard into its support only for it to catch and rock.
"Come on now. Come on!" She complained while she fussed with the weathered plank only realized laid a hard object, half covered to the lid with damp hay.
A-Qing rolled the board out of the slat with its rusted nails face up; her small hands dug around the slimy straw, button nose wrinkled when the stench of stale urine wafted from the hole in the floor. She soon excavated an odd clay pot. Twine looped a yellowed slip of parchment painted with red, encircled the jar, and bound the cork with a bow. On the surface of the jar, someone crudely etched a single symbol. A-Qing knew not what the slip or the logo meant, nor did she care. Curiosity became her, and she ripped off the hastily fastened tie.
Her inquisitive silver eyes peered into the jar, but the stench lurched her stomach. A-Qing's eyes watered, irritated, and she jerked the dress cuff to her nose, pinched off, and cupped over her mouth. Whatever the hell that was, it's foul. And she looked into the vase once more before the pot shattered at her feet with a screech.
Beige, corpulent globs of meat festered from the sour-smelling waste pooled upon the floor amongst the bits of ceramic. She tottered backward, the small of her back hit her bamboo pole that smacked the ground with a chilling clatter as she watched the unmovable mass of horror staring back at her.
"I see you've found something fun!" Suddenly spoke a voice behind A-Qing, who trembled, shocked. The stranger sidled into the morgue's coffin room as silent as a cat. His voice licked at a purr, almost as if he savored A-Qing's little blunder.
"Formaldehyde is so useful in preservation but so challenging to obtain. You wasted all my hard work!"
"You--" A-Qing's face burned red, and her skin licked goosebumps, and she wanted to run, hide, and scream. To find Daozhang. To tell him. To out this stranger. And the tongues. And.
"You're not going to breathe a single word of this to Daozhang or I will add your tongue to the pile on the floor. We will pretend nothing happened, and you will clean that shit up or I will end your miserable little life where you stand."
